# Benchmark reports mix revisions and are not reproducible

The report generator groups observations by workload and worker count while omitting revision. “Before” and “after” measurements are averaged into one series. The Fitch “scaling” benchmark also fixes Rayon to one worker, and committed paired/CPU charts are not generated by the checked-in reporter.

`def compute_metrics()` keys observations only by workload and jobs [dev/bench-graph-pass-report#L62-L94](../../dev/bench-graph-pass-report#L62-L94), even though the benchmark summary records a version field [dev/bench-graph-pass-cli#L268-L285](../../dev/bench-graph-pass-cli#L268-L285). It appends both revisions' wall time, memory, user time, system time, minimum, and maximum to the same bucket, then describes the averaged measurements as one binary run twice [dev/bench-graph-pass-report#L531-L545](../../dev/bench-graph-pass-report#L531-L545).

## Required observation key

Every sample must retain at least:

$$k=(r,w,d,j,n)$$

where $r$ is the revision, $w$ is the workload, $d$ is the dataset, $j$ is the worker count, and $n$ is the replicate identifier. Aggregation may remove $n$ only after grouping by the other dimensions.

## Options

- O1. Use typed records/dataframes for parsing and grouping, then Plotters for every committed chart.
- O2. Retain ad hoc dictionaries and raw SVG generation. This leaves dimension and escaping rules implicit.

Recommendation: O1. The schema must make revision omission structurally visible, and one generator must reproduce all checked-in reports.

## Recommendation

Use a typed observation schema and the workspace plotting stack so revision dimensions are mandatory and every committed report is reproducible.
